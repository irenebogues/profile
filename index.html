About Me

Ms. Irene BOGUES is a dedicated communications professional, youth strategist and an impassioned social advocate. She was originally from the Philippines, her inclusive and engaging civic work in the youth, the grassroots and environmental advocacy allows her to travel to Amsterdam and the European Union Parliament in Brussels for further strategic policy training and forge sustainable relationships with private and government consultants. Ms. Bogues believes that we’ve merely seen the initial swell of youth empowerment and activism, as young women and men and kids all over the world feel then exercise the power of their words and numbers, and that the best way to harness and focus the coming tide will be through the non-profit sector, with dedicated outreach and advocacy. She is the current New York City Chapter Lead of ChickTech, a not-for-profit organization dedicated to increase the number of girls and young women pursuing technology-based careers and retaining women in the technology. Graduated with high distinction in History, her Master’s Thesis entitled "Femininity in Pre-War Nueve Fuerza, a local newspaper in Cebu, Philippines) Advertisements." It elucidates the Philippines’ female media representation amidst parochialism and patriarchy before for the Second World War. She also has a Bachelor Degree major in History, concentrating in archival research and management, postmodern history and 17 th to 20 th century European history. Currently, she is enrolled in an intensive software engineering program in Columbia Engineering in the City of New York.
